02

Project Objective and Decision Context

Research Question
What is the comparative efficacy and safety of interventions used in moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease among patients with inadequate response to conventional therapy?

Population

  • Patients with moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease despite conventional first-line therapy

Outcomes

  • Clinical efficacy (e.g., remission, response, disease activity)
  • Safety (e.g., adverse events, serious adverse events)
  • Outcomes relevant for evidence synthesis and economic modelling

Purpose

  • Support NICE reimbursement submission
  • Provide evidence base for network meta-analysis
  • Inform economic model inputs

04

SLR Search Protocol

Element Criteria
P
 Patients with moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease with inadequate response to conventional therapy
I
 Relevant pharmacological interventions identified through scoping review and NICE guidance
C
 Relevant active treatments and placebo, where appropriate
O
 Clinical remission, Clinical response, Safety outcomes, Outcomes relevant to economic modelling
S
 Randomized controlled trials

Protocol Transparency

  • Protocol developed in accordance with PRISMA-P guidance
  • Registered in PROSPERO

Databases

MEDLINE Embase Cochrane

Study Period

  • No date restrictions initially

Search Concepts

  • Disease: Crohn's disease
  • Interventions: Relevant pharmacological therapies (Infliximab, Adalimumab, Vedolizumab, Ustekinumab, Risankizumab, Upadacitinib)
  • Study design: Randomized controlled trials
  • Search fields: MeSH terms; Free-text keywords; Synonyms and spelling variations
  • Full search string in the appendix
05

Study Selection

  • Studies imported into Covidence for screening and study management
  • Title/abstract screening against predefined eligibility criteria
  • Full-text review of potentially relevant studies
  • Dual independent screening to reduce selection bias
  • Study selection reported using a PRISMA flow diagram
06

Data Extraction & Quality Assessment

Data Extraction

  • Standardized extraction forms
  • Dual review and verification
  • Study characteristics, interventions, comparators, efficacy and safety outcomes
  • Outcomes relevant to economic modelling

Quality Assessment

  • Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2)
  • Independent assessment by two reviewers
  • Consensus-based discrepancy resolution
07

Evidence Synthesis & Meta-analysis Plan

Qualitative Synthesis

  • Summarize study and patient characteristics
  • Summarize efficacy and safety outcomes
  • Assess clinical and methodological heterogeneity

Quantitative Synthesis

  • Pairwise meta-analysis where appropriate
  • Network meta-analysis (NMA) if evidence network is feasible

Assessment of Heterogeneity

  • Study populations
  • Outcome definitions
  • Follow-up duration
  • Treatment history

Outputs

  • Comparative efficacy estimates
  • Comparative safety estimates
  • Inputs for economic modelling
